Waymo One

UX, UI, Motion

Designing for end consumers and testing with the early riders, I helped tackled a few pain points and usability issues that were unique to self-driving cars:
• Waymo self-driving cars can only stop at some areas of the street, likely not the spot users want to be picked up or dropped off
• There need to be explorations on saved location and walking instruction UI
• Users don’t understand why the car behave in certain ways
• Pedestrians gesture to the car 95% of the times, but the car does not have a way to communicate back
As a UX design intern, I worked heavily on visual and motion interaction of the pickup dropoff selection, saved locations, icon animations, rider screen HUD signals, car-ID hardware UX, and car-finding AR concept. During my time there, I collaborated with 1 visual designer and 2 front end engineers to implement the changes that later launched as Waymo One.
